Welcome to City of Santo Tomas

A first class component city in the province of Batangas, Philippines

"A city where heritage meets progress"

Brief Description

Nestled in the heart of Batangas province in the Philippines, the City of Santo Tomas is a vibrant and dynamic community known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and economic growth. Located approximately 85 kilometers south of Manila, Santo Tomas serves as a gateway to the southern regions of Luzon.

Brief History

Established in 1578, Santo Tomas has deep historical roots, originally named after Saint Thomas Aquinas. The city played a crucial role during the Spanish colonial period, evident in its historical architecture and landmarks. The San Juan Bautista Church, built in 1860, stands as a testament to the city’s rich ecclesiastical heritage.

Brief Economic Status

Santo Tomas is recognized as an emerging economic hub in Batangas, bolstered by its strategic location near major industrial parks, such as the Light Industry and Science Park and the First Philippine Industrial Park. The city is home to various businesses, from small enterprises to multinational corporations, contributing significantly to the local economy and providing ample job opportunities for residents.

Cultural Description

The residents of Santo Tomas, known as “Tomasinos,” are known for their warm hospitality and strong sense of community. The city hosts numerous festivals throughout the year, such as the “Pahiyas” and the “Santo Tomas City Fiesta,” celebrating local traditions, food, and culture.

Educational Description

With a focus on education, Santo Tomas is home to several reputable schools and institutions, catering to the growing population. The local government prioritizes infrastructure development, continuously improving roads, healthcare facilities, and public services to enhance the quality of life for its residents.

FEATURES:

Historical Landmarks

Tourists can explore various historical sites, such as the San Isidro Labrador Church, which features beautiful architecture and significant cultural heritage. The town's history is also showcased in local museums and heritage houses.

Natural Scenery

Santo Tomas offers stunning views of Mount Batulao, making it a great destination for hiking enthusiasts. The surrounding landscapes provide opportunities for outdoor activities like trekking and nature walks, attracting adventure seekers and nature lovers.

Culinary Delights

The town is known for its local delicacies and fresh produce. Visitors can enjoy authentic Batangas cuisine, including dishes like Bulalo (beef bone marrow soup) and Lomi (a thick noodle dish), as well as local snacks and desserts.

Proximity to Beach Resorts

While Santo Tomas itself is inland, it is conveniently located near popular beach destinations such as Nasugbu and Batangas City. Tourists can easily access beautiful beaches, resorts, and water activities, making it a perfect base for a beach getaway.
