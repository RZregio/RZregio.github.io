THE ALPS AND THE SEEKER

By: RZ Regio

"You're wondering what's beyond this alps,"

"You're wandering at the top of these cracks."

A strange but soft voice wakes the young boy,

Whose slumbering mind stirs from his dream.

"Follow my voice and make way in this tall labyrinth.

Take your feet and go by the wind,

Or keep on counting stars,

Waiting for eternal night from afar."

The strange but familiar voice suddenly disappears,

Leaving the young boy with a wordle that is crystal clear.

The boy stays silent for several minutes,

Observing the vast alps of titans in sight.

In his wandering, he finds a pathway to take,

He steps forward and makes haste.

An hour later, the boy rests beside a cave,

Sweat pouring down, and he hears the pulse of his veins.

Realizing the journey is no simple thing,

He stays in the cave and makes space to think.

A couple of nights spent dreaming,

A couple of days spent gathering and training.

When the sun awakens,

The boy resumes his hike and climbs up,

Confronting the benevolent call of the hilltop.

After weeks of perseverance,

He reaches the halfway point of his track.

He looks back, tracing memories left behind,

The dangers he encountered,

The knowledge and experiences that he grasped.

Looking forward,

The boy continues his journey,

Advancing though the path narrows,

Though what's ahead is shallow.

In the span of three strikes of the gong,

The young boy, with sparkling eyes and sweat,

Finds himself within reach of the mystery,

But there's no more path to walk.

Now, only the mountain's sheer face remains,

A steep climb, no more paths in sight.

With hands grasping, he begins his ascent,

Each move is a fight and a struggle of strength.

Higher and higher, his muscles ache,

But the summit calls, the boy must take

His final steps, the last of his climb,

His body strained, but his will is prime.

At last, he reaches the top,

Gasping for breath, his eyes wide in awe,

A new landscape unfolds before him,

But still beyond, more mountains to see.

And then, below, through a haze of clouds,

The boy see a sleeping sprout,

A mirror of himself, resting where he once lay,

Unaware of the journey he has yet to play.

Then looking forward, He takes a moment to breathe in the view,

Feeling the weight of his journey through.

He rests for a while, under the endless sky,

Before setting his sights on the next mountain high.
